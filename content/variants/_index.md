        **Do you or a family member have a genetic variant of uncertain significance in an ALS-associated gene?**

        Genetic testing for ALS has expanded dramatically in recent years. Many patients and families receive results showing a variant in a gene like TDP-43 (TARDBP), KIF5A, SETX, ERLIN2, VAPB, or dozens of other genes — but are told the variant's significance is unknown. This uncertainty is deeply frustrating when you are trying to understand a diagnosis or assess your own risk.

        The Brent Lab works to resolve this uncertainty. We study variants found in our patients and in families referred to us, characterizing what each variant does to the protein and to neurons. When we find a variant that affects protein function in ways consistent with disease, we report this to national databases (ClinVar) and work to update the clinical classification so that patients and their physicians have better information.

        ## Genes we currently study

        - **TARDBP (TDP-43)** — the most common cause of familial ALS-FTD
        - **KIF5A** — associated with ALS, hereditary spastic paraplegia, and a rare neonatal disorder
        - **SETX (senataxin)** — associated with ALS and a juvenile form of the disease
        - **ERLIN2** — associated with hereditary spastic paraplegia with potential conversion to ALS
        - **VAPB** — associated with a familial form of ALS first described in Brazilian families

        We are particularly interested in variants in families with multiple affected members, early disease onset, or unusual clinical presentations.

        ## How to contact us

        If you have a genetic variant you would like us to evaluate, please contact us at brentj@pitt.edu with the subject line "Variant inquiry." Please include the gene name and variant, the clinical diagnosis, and whether other family members are affected.

        We will respond to all inquiries. In some cases we may ask for additional clinical information, medical records, or a referral to our neuromuscular clinic at UPMC. Participation in research is always voluntary and there is no cost to families.

        ---

        *This page is for research inquiries. We are not able to provide clinical diagnoses or medical advice through this website. For urgent medical questions, please contact your physician or the UPMC neurology department directly.*
