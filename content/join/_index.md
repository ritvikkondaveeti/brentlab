        We are actively recruiting scientists who want to work at the intersection of cell biology, neuroscience, and human genetics. Our lab combines mechanistic cell biology with direct clinical discovery — the variants we study come from patients we see in clinic. If that approach excites you, we want to hear from you.

        ## Postdoctoral positions — two open now

        We are recruiting two postdoctoral associates to join a growing research program on ALS and motor neuron disease.

        **Position 1** focuses on the functional characterization of patient-derived genetic variants — TDP-43, KIF5A, ERLIN2, SETX, and VAPB — using cell-based assays, live imaging, and patient-derived iPSC models. Experience in cell biology, protein biochemistry, or neuroscience is ideal. Familiarity with iPSC culture is a plus but not required.

        **Position 2** focuses on kinesin biology and axonal transport, including a project comparing ALS and NEIMY disease mechanisms downstream of KIF5A mutations. Experience in live-cell imaging, motor protein biology, or neuronal cell culture is ideal.

        Both positions are funded and available now. Salary follows the NIH postdoctoral pay scale. The lab is located in the Biomedical Science Tower 3 at the University of Pittsburgh with access to state-of-the-art imaging, proteomics, and iPSC core facilities.

        **To apply:** send a CV, a brief description of your research interests and why our work interests you, and contact information for three references to brentj@pitt.edu. Subject line: Postdoc application. Applications are reviewed on a rolling basis.

        ## Graduate students

        We welcome rotation students from the University of Pittsburgh neuroscience (CNUP) and biomedical sciences graduate programs. Rotations are a mutual evaluation — we want to find the right fit for both the student and the lab. Please reach out to discuss current projects before applying for a rotation.

        ## What we offer

        A funded, growing research program with clear scientific direction. Direct access to a clinical neuromuscular practice — the variants we study come from patients we know. A collaborative environment with connections to groups at Oxford, Brown, JAX, Northwestern, and UT Southwestern. Mentorship oriented toward your next career step, whether that is faculty, industry, or clinical research. Pittsburgh is an affordable, livable city with excellent access to the northeast and midwest.
